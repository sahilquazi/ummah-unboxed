What This Is Really About

These businesses didn't lose the ability to make things. They lost access to buyers.

In Sudan, Palestine, Lebanon, and Iran, skilled craftspeople are still producing hand-woven keffiyehs, carved wooden art, embroidered textiles, and handcrafted jewelry. Wartime economics wipe out local demand — not skill.

Ummah Unboxed gives them a direct channel to US consumers. We take a small service fee from box sales and run the marketplace on minimal commission so artisans keep as much of their revenue as possible.

April's Featured Maker

Ibrahim Nasser — Bethlehem, Palestine

Palestine · Bethlehem · Woodwork & Calligraphy

He learned to carve wood before he learned to write. Now he does both at once.

Ibrahim has been creating hand-burned Arabic calligraphy artwork in his Bethlehem workshop since he was a teenager. Each piece features freehand calligraphy intertwined with floral motifs — burned into oak with a heated stylus. No machines, no stencils. His work turns prayers into art you can hang on your wall and touch with your hands.

"When I burn these words into wood, I am putting something permanent into the world. Paper burns. Wood endures."

This month's box includes his hand-burned calligraphy artwork — approximately 6 hours of work per piece. No two are identical.
